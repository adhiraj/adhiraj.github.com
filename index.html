Multi-label Naive-Bayes Text Classifier
Oct-Nov '12
Here at NUS we have a community called NUS Hackers, a group of people interested in breaking building and learning new things. We maintain a website similar to hackernews for us to share articles about content with timeless value – algorithms, programming paradigms, research, culture etc., as opposed to things that are just trending, like most articles on hackernews. When we submit articles to the site, we can tag them with categories, and my idea was to recommend tags automatically based on a naive Bayes model trained on a dataset of 100K user-tagged links submitted on delicious. It will remain an academic exercise because delicious has an API for tag suggestions that leverages its network and user tags and performs far better than any naive Bayes model would be able to in practice. The process of crawling and cleaning thousands of documents and tweaking the model was so interesting that I've documented it in my blog.
iMenus
Digital menu systems for restaurants
Mar-May '11
An iPad app we made for an epic course (CS3217) at NUS. Basically it was a digital menu system for restaurants complete with POS integration. Customers order on iPads placed on the tables, and stewards/chefs can see the orders pouring in on their kitchen system. After a meal, customers can call for the bill that would be sent to the POS system. A lot like POS Lavu. Unfortunately we college kids were too busy doing our college-y stuff and the team disintegrated after the semester so we left it at that.
Huff & Puff
Angry Birds Reinvented
Jan-Feb '11
Huff & Puff is a cute little Windows Mobile game that I reinvented for the iPad. It's a hybrid between the kindergarten story The Three Little Pigs and Rovio's Angry Birds. You create game levels by placing blocks of varying strengths and sizes and constructing structures to protect the pigs. Then you take on the role of the evil wolf that can puff in many different ways and try to kill the pigs. We coded most of the game from scratch, including the physics engine.

Sportup!
Matchmaking for people looking for sports players at any time
Mar-Apr '12
So a tennis playing friend of ours was facing a problem – often he wanted to play tennis but couldn't immediately find a partner either because his near and dear ones all don't play tennis, or the times they're free don't match, or there was no good way of discovering random strangers interested in playing at that time too. Thus was born Sportup! Just key in your location and see who's playing what in your vicinity, and who's looking for people to play with. You can also schedule future games and friend people on the site (of course, how can a web app be complete without social?). Done using the epic Ruby on Rails framework.
